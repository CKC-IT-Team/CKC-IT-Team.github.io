Ranking Information
The military division operates on a merit-based hierarchical system with an elected administrator (as with any other division). Rankings and their assignments are defined directly by said administrator.
Rankings, in order of senority, are as follows:
Generals (Higher Administrators)
General
Personal advisor and direct subordinate of division administrator. Highest ranking official of our military with the exception of the administrator, and thus is permitted to issue direct orders to any subordinate.
Lieutenant General
Personal executives of generals; permitted to issue direct orders and required to issue orders from their general.
Major General
Lower-ranked personal executives of generals; permitted to issue direct orders and required to issue orders from their general.
Brigadier General
Lowest-ranked general - should hold lower management positions. Permitted to issue direct orders and most likely direct a group of subordinates.
Lower Administrators
Colonel
Direct advisor and subordinate of their general. Highest ranking field unit of our military, and thus is permitted to issue direct orders to any subordinate but also required to participate in operations.
Lieutenant Colonel
Operates in place of a colonel when a colonel is not available. Permitted to act as a colonel unless one arrives to relieve them.
Major
Handles lower administrative work, but does operate in the field under their colonel. Permitted to issue orders if necessary or to go about their administrative work.
Squad Leaders
Captain
Highest-ranked and most experienced squad leader. Permitted to issue orders to anyone in their squad regardless of rank.
First Lieutenant
More experienced squad leader. Permitted to issue orders to anyone in their squad regardless of rank.
Second Lieutenant
Lowest-ranked and least experienced squad leader. Permitted to issue orders to anyone in their squad with regard to rank.
Squad Members
Sergeant Major of the Military Corps
Highest-ranked and most experienced squad member. Permitted to temporarily take up the role (but not title) of 2nd lieutenant as squad leader if necessary.
Sergeant Major
Very experienced squad member. Permitted to temporarily take up the role (but not title) of 2nd lieutenant as squad leader if necessary and no SMMC exists to do so.
Master Gunnery Sergeant
Highest-ranked specialist, assigned to one specialty by their CO.
First Sergeant
Highest-ranked non-specialist. May be temporarily assigned to a specialty by their CO, with permanent assignments warranting promotion.
Master Sergeant
Direct subordinate to first sergeant, acting in their place if necessary. May not take up a particular specialty bar promotion.
Gunnery Sergeant
Lower-ranked specialist. Assigned to a Master Gunnery Sergeant to help execute their specialty.
Staff Sergeant
Exists solely to regulate other squad members, regardless of rank - takes orders from a higher-ranked squad member or lower-ranked squad leader.
Sergeant
Permitted to issue orders to corporals, lance corporals and privates.
Corporal
Lower-ranked squad member. Operates under a sergeant.
Lance Corporal
Lowest-ranked squad member. Operates under a sergeant.
Corpsmen
Private 1st Class
A corpsman almost ready to be promoted to a squad member. Handles menial tasks and is not permitted to issue orders.
Private
A fresh corpsman; entry rank. Handles menial tasks and is not permitted to issue orders.
